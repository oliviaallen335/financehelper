Financial Statements Review
Balance Sheet.
- Financial statement that shows the value of the firm’s assets and liabilities at a particular time
- Measured from an accounting perspective
- Assets (use of funds) are listed on the left
- Liabilities + Equity (source of funds) are listed on the right

Balance Sheet Areas
Current assets
- short term investments, hurt cash flow
- Cash and marketable securities, accounts receivable, inventory, etc.
Current liabilities
- help cash flow, future cash outflow
- Accounts payable, notes payable
Net working capital = Current assets – current liabilities

Balance Sheet Areas
Long-term assets
- Tangible (property, plant and equipment) and intangible (goodwill)
- Accumulated depreciation (Gross vs net PPE)
Long-term liabilities (debt)
Equity
- Common stock vs Additional paid-in capital
- Retained earnings

Book vs Market Value?
Book value
- Balance sheet valuations
- Book values look backwards
Market value
- The value of assets or liabilities if resold in a market.
- Forward looking, consider the prospect of future cash flows
What about intangible assets?

Market Value Measurements
Market Capitalization
- Minimum value for equity of company
- # shares outstanding * current stock price
Enterprise value
- Total market value of the firm
- Market value of Equity + Market value of Debt - Cash

Profit vs Cash Flow
It is important to note that accounting profit does not imply the firm has that value of cash on hand. Recall, 1. Depreciation
EBIT = total revenues – costs - depreciation

Financial Statement Review
Statement of Cash Flows
- Financial statement that shows the firm’s cash receipts and cash payments over a period of time
- It starts with profits and converts to cash flows

Financial Statement Review
Statement of Cash Flows
- Cash flow from Operations (Net Income + Depreciation +/- etc.)
- Cash flow from Investments (change in PPE)
- Cash flow from Financing (notes payable, long-term debt, dividends)
- Cash reconciliation

Free Cash Flow
Free Cash Flow (FCF)
- Cash available for distribution to investors after firm pays for new investments or additions to working capital.
