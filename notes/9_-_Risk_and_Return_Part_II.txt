Diversification
Diversification is a strategy designed to reduce risk by spreading the portfolio across many investments.
- By dividing up investments across many relatively low-correlated assets, it is possible to considerably reduce one’s exposure to risk
- Markowitz (1959), “Portfolio Selection”, Journal of Finance

Diversification Benefit
Idiosyncratic/Diversifiable risk:
- Related to asset (company) specific events/uncertainty
- Also called firm-specific risk
Systematic/Non-diversifiable risk:
- Economy-wide sources of risk that affect the overall stock market
- Also called market risk
Well-diversified portfolio: one whose specific risk has been eliminated

The
“Market
Portfolio”
Diversification benefit leads an investor to build the most well diversified portfolio possible, i.e., the market portfolio

Portfolio Beta
We can measure the risk of a well-diversified portfolio by measure the beta of the overall portfolio
- Aids in determining performance (return) relative to risk of a portfolio
- See how each asset contributes to the overall risk of the portfolio

Capital Asset Pricing Model
The expected rate of return on an investment depends upon two things:
- Basic compensation for waiting (risk-free return)
- An investment-specific risk premium
- CAPM models the “well diversified” investors trade-off between risk and return (the required return based upon portfolio risk)

Example 2:
Let’s say that you are looking at investing in 2 stocks A and B. A has a beta of 1.3 and is predicted to have a return of 15%, B has a beta of 0.9 and is predicted to earn 9%. If the risk- free rate is currently 4% and the expected return on the market is 11%, determine whether these stocks are worth investing in.

Stock A’s expected/required return = 4% + (11%-4%)*1.3 = 13.1%
Stock B’s expected/required return = 4% + (11%-4%)*0.9 = 10.3%
Stock A would, since 15%>13.1% would be considered undervalued (it offers a higher return that required), while stock B would be considered overvalued (9%<10.3%) since it offers a lower return than required.
