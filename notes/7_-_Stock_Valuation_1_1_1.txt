Characteristics of Stock
Major financing vehicle for corporations
- Provides holders with an opportunity to share in the future cash flows of the issuer.
- Holders have “ownership” in the company (residual claim on profit)
- Unlike bonds, no maturity date and variable periodic income

Stock Terminology
Common stock
- First sold (initial public offering or IPO) in the primary markets
- Most investors trade stock in the secondary market
- Stocks may or may not issue a dividend (managerial decision)
- Frequently carry a voting right most notably regarding Board of Directors

Stock Terminology
Preferred stock
- No ownership or voting right
- Fixed dividend payment (either amount or percentage)
- No maturity (like common stock)

Preferred Stock
Preferred stock becomes a perpetuity, We can also use this if we consider the firm a “no-growth” firm, where 1) the firm re-invests no profit, and 2) the firm pays out all profit as dividend (pay out EPS)

Deriving g
A sustainable growth rate is achieved by a firm re-investing its earnings while keeping long-term debt ratios constant
- Earnings can either issued as dividends (payout) or kept as retained earnings (plowed back)
- If earnings are retained, they would theoretically return ROE

Example 3:
Aqua America has an ROE of 12.5% and is expected to pay a dividend of $0.86. It intends to plowback 38% of its earnings and the opportunity cost of capital is 7.0%. What is the stock price?

0.07−0.0475 = 38.23

Deriving g (continued)
