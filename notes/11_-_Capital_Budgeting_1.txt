Internal Rate of Return
The rate of return implied by a project’s cost and future cash flows
- IRR must be calculated
- IRR can also be called the discounted cash-flow (DCF) rate of return
- IRR rule: If IRR > opportunity cost of capital, accept the project; if
IRR < opportunity cost of capital, reject the project

IRR and NPV
- IRR is the discount rate that sets NPV equal to zero
- If IRR > opportunity cost of capital, generally NPV is positive; if IRR
< opportunity cost of capital, generally NPV is negative

NPV Profile
An NPV profile shows the NPV of an investment with different discount rates.

Special Cases
Case 1: Mutually exclusive projects
- With NPV, choosing project with highest NPV is often the best choice (but not always)
- With IRR, choosing the highest IRR can lead to poor decision making
- Pay attention to either the timing of cash flows (projects have different lives) or large differences in outlays

Special Cases
Case 2: Lending or Borrowing
- Rule for IRR: NPV must be falling
- Not the case when lending and borrowing (positive initial CF, negative future CFs)
Case 3: Multiple IRRs
- Occurs with non-normal CFs (change in sign of CF during stream)

Modified IRR
Modified IRR takes the cashflows of a project and “reinvests” them at the firm’s cost of capital
- Avoids unrealistic return assumptions of traditional IRR
- Assumes firm will earn its cost of capital

Profitability Index
NPV per dollar invested in a project
- Sometimes called the Benefit-cost ratio
- With a limited amount of capital and multiple projects, PI can be used as a ranking/decision criteria

Payback Period
Time until cash flows recover the initial investment of a project
- Accept a project if its payback period is less than pre-determined, specified cutoff period
- Ideal for small, short-term decision making
- Does not consider any projects after the payback period nor does it use TVM principles

Discounted Payback Period
Time until PRESENT VALUE of cash flows recover the initial investment of a project
- Each cash flow from the project is discounted to present value
- Incorporates TVM principals but still does not consider any projects after the payback period
