Corporate Performance
Traditionally, measuring corporate performance (health) is done through financial ratios
- relationships between different accounts from financial statements—usually the income statement and the balance sheet— that serve as performance indicators
- Time, industry and objective
- Make sure you understand the “story” the ratios are telling

Shareholder Value
How much value does the firm generate?
Market-to-book ratio
P/E ratio
Investment
How profitable is the firm?
Total Cap
ROC, ROA, ROE
Financing
Can the firm finance future growth?
Efficient use of assets?
Turnover ratios for assets, inventory and receivables
Profits from sales?
Operating profit margin
Prudent financial leverage?
Debt ratios
Interest coverage ratios
Sufficient liquidity?
Current, quick and cash ratios
