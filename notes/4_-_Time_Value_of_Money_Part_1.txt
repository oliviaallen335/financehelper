Present Value and Discounting
The value of a future cash flow at the start of the stated period is called the present or discounted value of that sum
- Involves discounting the interest that would have been earned over a given period at a given interest rate
- Exact opposite of future value or compounding
- Useful in determining the value (or price) of an asset that produces cash flows in the future
